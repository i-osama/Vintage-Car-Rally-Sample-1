All the stages of the event

PRE-EVENT

The navigator is usually responsible for looking after the pre-event documentation and helping the driver get the car registered at the event. The navigator should collect the road book and time cards and any other instructions supplied by the organisers. He/she should run through the road book to highlight any areas where they may expect time controls to be located.

AT THE START

Navigators should ensure that the driver has the car warmed-up and ready well before your due time at the first control. Different events may have different formats. You may have the control sited inside a building or on the road just before the start, after your time has been filled in, the navigator must check that the time is accurate and that the marshal has put it in the right place.
